Proverbs


Cross the stream where it is the shallowest: This proverb means to take the easiest or safest path when facing a challenge. Instead of making things harder for yourself, find the simplest solution.
Sentence: When you have a difficult homework assignment, cross the stream where it is the shallowest by starting with the easiest questions first.

After the feast comes the reckoning: This proverb means that after enjoying something, there are often consequences or responsibilities to deal with. It reminds us that fun times are usually followed by work or responsibility.
Sentence: We had a great time at the party, but after the feast comes the reckoning when it's time to clean up.
